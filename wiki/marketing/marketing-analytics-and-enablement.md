# Marketing Analytics And Enablement

> Sources: Anthropic growth-marketing article, Eric Siu AI Marketing Skills README, marketing analytics Last30Days sweep, performance and content workflow pages.

## Overview

Marketing analytics appears in two layers. First, every marketing workflow needs a campaign measurement loop: creative performance, SEO visibility, landing-page conversion, UGC variant performance, lifecycle conversion, pipeline influence, and attribution. Second, AI-enabled marketing teams need enablement analytics: which workflows changed, which tools were reused, which quality gates worked, and which marketers became more self-sufficient.

That means marketing analytics is not only dashboards for campaigns. It is also a dashboard for organizational capability: time saved, review quality, reuse, adoption, and whether a workflow can keep improving without the original builder in the loop.

Eric Siu's repo gives the marketing-performance side: Growth Engine, Weekly Scorecard, Revenue Intelligence, Revenue Attribution, Client Report Generator, Experiment Engine, and statistical experiment methods. Anthropic's examples show the artifact side: faster ad creation, content workflows, and enablement outputs need review and downstream measurement.

## Analytics Layers

- **Campaign analytics:** spend, impressions, clicks, conversions, pipeline, revenue, attribution, content visibility, landing-page conversion, and lifecycle movement.
- **Experiment analytics:** hypothesis, variable, variants, sample, metric, guardrails, confidence, result, decision, and next test.
- **Workflow analytics:** baseline time, current time, error/rework rate, adoption, reuse, review quality, and output acceptance.
- **Cohort enablement analytics:** marketer maturity, first wins, regular use, self-build ability, transformed workflows, shared patterns, and leadership-visible wins.
- **Governance analytics:** approval latency, policy failures, hallucination/factual issues, stale sources, and tool side effects.

## Enablement Dashboard Sketch

A marketing enablement dashboard should answer:

- Which workflows were identified, built, adopted, and scaled?
- Which workflows have source files, runbooks, owners, and review gates?
- How many marketers moved from awareness to first win, regular AI integration, full workflow transformation, and self-sufficiency?
- Which tools/skills were reused by peers outside the original user?
- Which workflows still depend on the original builder versus marketer-owned iteration?
